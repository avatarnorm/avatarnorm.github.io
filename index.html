Methodology

To achieve high-quality and realistic 3D human generation, we propose AvatarNorm. The core of our approach lies in intergrating the geometry prior of 3D human into the existing 2D diffusion model. First, we collect an extensive 3D human dataset and intergrate geometry prior into 2D diffusion model by finetuning. The trained geometry-aware diffusion model excels in generating multi-view depth maps and normal maps of humans. Additionally, to ensure high-quality texture generation, we train a geometry-guided controlnet that efficiently integrates the geometry information of humans into the RGB generation process. Building upon the foundation of the geometry-aware diffusion model, we introduce a high-quality 3D human body generation framework.


The core of this framework comprises two main components. The first part focuses on human body geometry generation, utilizing a progressive geometric optimization approach to fully leverage the prior information from the geometry-aware diffusion model. After geometric optimization is completed, a coarse-to-fine texture optimization based on the geometry-guided diffusion model is employed to generate textures, resulting in a high-quality three-dimensional human body model.

Societal Impact

The objective of this project is to equip users with a powerful tool for creating realistic 3D avatars. Our strategy allows users to generate 3D avatars based on their specific prompts. However, there is a potential risk that these generated models could be misused by malicious entities to deceive viewers, a problem that is not unique to our approach but is prevalent in other generative model methodologies and content manipulation techniques. Therefore, it is imperative for ongoing and future research in generative modeling to continually address and reevaluate these concerns.
